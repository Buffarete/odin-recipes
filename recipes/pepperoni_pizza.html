Pepperoni Pizza

Home

Pepperoni pizza is an all-time favorite that combines a crispy, chewy crust with tangy tomato sauce, melted mozzarella cheese, and spicy, flavorful slices of pepperoni. This crowd-pleaser originated in the United States but draws inspiration from traditional Italian pizza-making. The bold pepperoni slices crisp up deliciously around the edges as they bake, infusing the pizza with a smoky depth of flavor. Whether homemade or ordered from your favorite pizzeria, pepperoni pizza is the perfect comfort food for any occasion.


Ingredients

  • 1 ball of pizza dough (about 250-300g or enough for one 12-inch pizza)
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) tomato pizza sauce
  • 200g (7 oz) shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 100g (3.5 oz) pepperoni slices
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Optional: dried oregano or chili flakes for extra flavor

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature it can reach (usually about 475-500°F or 250-260°C). If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat.
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a lightly floured surface to your desired thickness, typically about 12 inches in diameter.
  3. Transfer the rolled dough to a pizza peel or baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.
  5. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese evenly over the sauce.
  6. Arrange the pepperoni slices on top of the cheese, distributing them generously.
  7. Drizzle a little olive oil over the pizza and sprinkle optional dried oregano or chili flakes if you like.
  8. Carefully slide the pizza onto the preheated pizza stone or place the baking sheet in the oven.
  9. Bake for about 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and crispy, and the cheese is bubbling and slightly browned.
  10. Remove the pizza from the oven, let it cool for a couple of minutes, slice, and serve hot.
